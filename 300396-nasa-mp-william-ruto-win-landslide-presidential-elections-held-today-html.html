NASA MP says William Ruto would win with landslide if presidential elections were held today

March 2024 Â· 4 minute read

- MP Chris Wamalwa said Ruto had a better chance of wining the presidency compared to any of his competitors

- The legislator challenged president Uhuru Kenyatta to keep his promise of supporting the DP in 2022

- Wamalwa claimed there were signs Jubilee would betray Ruto in 2022

Kiminini MP Chris Wamalwa has claimed Deputy President William Ruto would floor all his opponents if presidential elections were held today.

Wamalwa who was elected on a FORD Kenya ticket under the National Super Alliance (NASA) coalition said the more a section of Jubilee leaders were waging war against Ruto's ambitions, the more he was gaining support from Kenyans.

In a video clip seen by TUKO.co.ke, on Monday, March 11, the National Assembly Minority Chief Whip accused President Uhuru Kenyatta of reneging on his promise to support Ruto after his second term ends in 2022.

"The president said clearly that he would rule for 10 years then back his deputy William Ruto for another 10 years, but were are seeing signs of betrayal. It seems the president is distancing himself from that promise which is not good at all," said Wamalwa

In the wake of the fight against graft in the country which has seen Ruto and Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga engage in nasty verbal attacks, the Opposition legislator argued the fight had been designed to scuttle the DP's presidential ambitions.

"Kenyans are getting sympathetic of Ruto and I can assure you if elections are held today, William Ruto will be the president. Nobody can stop that which God has blessed, no amount of scandals will scuttle Ruto's ambitions," he said

The FORD Kenya legislator asked the Luhya community to back the DP's bid arguing that he had demonstrated good leadership skills and was development oriented.

Wamalwa also said Ruto, who hails from Uasin Gishu county was closer to Mulembe nation and would serve their interests best compared to his other competitors.

"Ruto is our neighbor, if we elect him president, he will definitely consider our region for development," he said.

With Amani National Congress (ANC) leader Musalia Mudavadi and his Ford Kenya counterpart Moses Wetang'ula warming up for the 2022 presidential race, Wamalwa's public endorsement of Ruto's presidential bid leaves Mulembe nation's quest for the country's top seat in a precarious position.

The DP's political influence in western Kenya region has been gaining momentum and a number of legislators have openly declared support for his bid.

In Bungoma county where FORD Kenya party leader Wetang'u la hails from, Ruto has secured the support of John Waluke (Sirisia), Didmus Barasa (Kimilili), Dan Wanyama (Webuye West), Mwambu Mabonga (Bumula) and Fred Kapondi (Mt Elgon).

In Kakamega county, the DP could be banking on defiant FORD Kenya deputy party leader Boni Khalwale and Mumias East legislator Benard Washiali to blow his trumpet.

With Wamalwa visibly on the verge of joining his camp, Ruto's 2022 political ambitions could be characterised with a lot of push and pull whose only outcome could be told by the Kenyan voter through the ballot.
