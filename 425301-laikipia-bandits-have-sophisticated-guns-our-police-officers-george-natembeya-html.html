As the security situation in Laikipia appears to be getting out of hand, it is now emerging that bandits who have been terrorising residents in recent days are armed with sophisticated firearms.

Rift Valley Regional Coordinator George Natembeya while leading an operation at the Laikipia Nature Conservancy on Tuesday, September 7, said the bandits own guns that cannot be matched by those used by the Kenyan police officers.

Sophisticated M16 guns

He said that some of the weapons, like the M16 guns, are owned by foreign troops who conduct paramilitary training in the region.

Laikipia is a vast but harsh place used for the joint Kenya Defence Forces and British Army training and hosting several wildlife conservation ranches.

''The guns that these bandits are using are mighty. They are called M16. Most of them are only used by foreign troops who come to train in Kenya,'' Natembeya told journalists.

An M16 rifle, used by the military only, is a 223 calibre (5.56 millimetre) gas-operated magazine-fed rifle for semiautomatic or automatic operation.

“How such a powerful rifle got into their hands is a puzzle that should be investigated,” Natembeya added.

Volatile Laikipia region

Laikipia is a landmine waiting to explode and could be described as a face of many other parts of Kenya, where historical injustices have never been addressed, the land being at the centre stage.

The recent attacks have left at least seven people dead and hundreds homeless.

Hundreds of villagers have fled their homes as bandits stage more attacks.

The bandits have been torching homes, schools despite the ongoing security operation in the area.

According to Natembeya, the law nowadays gives criminals leeway to get away with anything.

Police deployment in Laikipia

The administrator said the government couldn't deploy security officers across the vast insecurity-prone region.

''All I can say is that we cannot put police officers everywhere. These people are launching their attacks sporadically to the extent that they do not have a particular formula or pattern they are using. They check where the officers are not stationed, and that is where they attack,'' he said.

This comes as a primary school in the troubled Olmoran area on Tuesday, September 7, was set on fire by the suspected bandits.

The incident came barely a day after the National Security Advisory Council declared the volatile Laikipia Nature Conservancy its environs a security operation zone.
