High Court declines to suspend governments decision to cancel betting licenses

February 2024 Â· 2 minute read

- Betting board had suspended licenses of at least 27 gaming firms in the country over failure to comply with tax obligations

- Further, the government suspended the firms' pay bill and SMS numbers

- The companies moved to court arguing there was an order in place that allowed them to continue operating

- They requested the court to be given conservatory orders to continue operating

The High Court has declined to suspend government's decision to blacklist 27 betting and gambling companies over failure to comply with tax obligations.

On Wednesday, July 10, the Betting Control and Licensing Board boss Liti Wambua suspended licences of at least 27 betting firms from operating in Kenya until they are tax compliant.

Wambua also directed all mobile money transfer service providers to withdraw their pay bill numbers used by affected companies.

At least four firms had approached the court seeking conservatory orders against government to allow them to continue their businesses.

However, on Wednesday, July 17, Justice Weldon Korir said that the court did not find just cause to issue the orders before BCLB and other government agencies file responses.

In the suit, the companies argued that the switch-off of the M-Pesa pay bills and SMS short codes was illegal as they had court orders allowing them to continue operating despite being denied licences by the government.

The firms wanted the court to lift suspension of their payment systems and have top officials of BCLB jailed or fined for ordering the switch-off despite the court order.

The case was scheduled for hearing on July 26 when the respondents shall have filed their replies.

On Tuesday, July 16, Interior CS Fred Matiang'i signed deportation orders for foreign directors of the affected companies.
