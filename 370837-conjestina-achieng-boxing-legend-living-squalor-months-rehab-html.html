Conjestina Achieng: Boxing legend still living in squalor months after rehab

Boxing legend Conjestina Achieng appears to still be living in squalor despite Nairobi governor Mike Sonko promising to have her rehabilitated.

The sorry state of Conjestina's life initially came to the fore back in October 2018 after a video of her emerged suggesting she had been abandoned as she battled a mental condition.

Conjestina's plight attracted the attention of Sonko, who consequently offered to have her rehabilitated.

The pioneering Kenyan boxing queen was eventually airlifted to Nairobi for treatment, with Sonko, at the time divulging she would undergo a comprehensive check up before admission to a rehabilitation centre.

After months of treatment, Congestina evidenced glimpses of progress as she worked her way to full recovery.

So much progress that in March 2019 she made a rare appearance during Fatuma Zarika's triumph over Zambian opponent Catherine Phiri.

Conjestina formed part of the audience that cheered Zarika to victory and was even seen making her way into the ring moments after the match.

However, the 42-year-old seems to have regressed and returned to her former ways if a recent account on her by radio personality, Carol Radull, is anything to go by.

Radull, who paid her a visit at her rural Siaya home revealed on Tuesday, July 28, the Hands of Stone is struggling.

The sports host disclosed the mother of one has lost weight but still looks physically strong.

"In my layman's assessment; she is not mad but she does need more rehab to overcome her dependence on abusive substances. She also needs some counselling (anger management). But she then needs to be in an environment where her mind is kept busy; where her talent is being used; where she feels useful and appreciated," she posted."We all just hope and pray for a lasting solution that will see such an icon be in a position to make an impact towards the sport of boxing in this country. She still can," she added.
