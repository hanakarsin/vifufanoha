Meru Governor Kiraitu Murungi has excited hustlers after he used a wheelbarrow during the launch of the county's youth empowerment drive.

Photos shared by the first term county boss showed him collecting trash and placing it on the small cart as he launched a cleanup exercise that he said will target over 600 youths.

"We continue with our pledge of empowering county youths through the ng'arish mtaa initiative which we have launched today at Mitunguu Market under the Meru Youth Service programme.

Ruto's charm offensive

Ruto, who has set his eyes on the 2022 presidency, has been charming the youth with his charity work and donations allegedly aimed at empowering them economically.

The second in command has been donating equipment including wheelbarrows worth millions as he tries to woo young people who form the majority of voters to his side ahead of the upcoming General Election.

A section of leaders has, however, accused the DP of perpetuating poverty in the country by giving out "useless" outdated machines and tools with little economic impact.

A week ago, Kitui Governor Charity Ngilu challenged Ruto to donate the items to his children if he was really serious about empowering Kenyans.

The county boss said the youth should be given skills and shown the market and they would, in turn, be able to prosper and have a sustainable life.
