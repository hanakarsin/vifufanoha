NTSA says motorist caught on camera in opposite lane was rushing woman in labour to hospital

May 2024 Â· 2 minute read

- The public service vehicle was spotted along Langata road on Wednesday, October 2

- Photo of the dangerously overlapping vehicle was shared online

- NTSA confirmed it was taking an expectant woman to Mbagathi Hospital

It has now emerged traffic officers allowed a public service vehicle that was caught on camera driving on the opposite lane along Lang'ata road on Wednesday, October 2, do so.

The regulator, the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) confirmed the vehicle, registration number KCG 922 C, was actually rushing to Mbagathi Hospital.

It carried an expectant woman who according to the authority, seriously needed emergency services at the time of the incident as she was in labour.

"We have confirmed that the said vehicle was carrying an expectant mother as an emergency to Mbagathi Hospital who was about to deliver and had the blessings of the traffic police manning the roundabout, DTO Lang'ata has confirmed and Mbagathi Hospital through a letter," the NTSA said.

Photo of the dangerously driven PSV was published online by an internet user by the name Earl Gib.

"This is what happens to people who know that nothing is going to happen. Because 1'm sure this guy knew that they will be taken a video or a pic but he still did this.. From carnivore to Weston hotel," he tweeted, prompting NTSA to launch investigations.
