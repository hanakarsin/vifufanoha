Khaligraph Jones Says Meeting with William Ruto Has Led to Easing of COVID-19 Regulations

August 2024 · 3 minute read

- Khaligraph Jones took to social media and bragged about his efforts in ensuring the government opens the country

- The rapper and other local celebrities had earlier met Deputy President William Ruto and discussed unlocking Kenya

- President Uhuru Kenyatta on Saturday, May 1, eased a number of COVID-19 regulations in a move that excited many Kenyans

Kenyan rapper Brian Omollo, known to many as Khaligraph Jones is in celebratory moods after President Uhuru Kenyatta eased some of the COVID-19 regulations.

Khali joined millions of Kenyans in celebrations just hours after Uhuru lifted cessation of movement in Nairobi, Kiambu, Nakuru, Kajiado and Machakos.

The president, while addressing the nation during the Labour Day celebrations at the State House in Nairobi, observed a significant decrease in the COVID-19 positivity rate in the zoned area.

Consequently, the head of state announced Public Order No 3 of 2021, which essentially lifted cessation of movement in and out of the zone area effective Saturday, May 1, midnight.

Uhuru also noted that the curfew hours in the zoned area will begin from 10pm, and end at 4am and not between 8pm and 4am.

These and other eased regulations impressed many Kenyans including Khaligraph who somehow took credit for the same.

Taking to his Instagram account, the rapper said his meeting with Deputy President William Ruto had something to do with Uhuru's move today.

According to the rapper, it was not what he and other artistes who were in the meeting expected but was glad it happened.

In his usual demeanour, the OG called on Kenyans to camp on the comments section of his post to thank him and respect the OG.
