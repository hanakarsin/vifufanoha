Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha has rubbished a report by the Kenya National Examinations Council (KNEC) which revealed Class Eight candidates had massively failed in their school based exams upon resumption of learning.

The no nonsense CS said the survey would "kill" the spirit of children whom he termed as intelligent and hardworking.

Speaking in Nairobi on Wednesday, January 3, Magoha expressed his confidence that learners will post improved performance in the forthcoming Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) exams as he dismissed fears of unpreparedness.

"We should have contextualized this report in such a manner that it was done more than 12 weeks ago. Since then, the learners have been in school and they are going to be there for another six weeks before they have exams. What the hell do you think you are doing by killing their spirit?""Those children are intelligent. All we should do is to give them hope in their exams and life and see how far they go," Magoha said.

The former KNEC chairman said he was confident learning interventions by teachers since resumption of learning had helped to recover the loss detected through the assessments.

Concern over massive failure

The Kenya National Examinations Council had expressed concern over KCPE candidates' massive failure in school tests with only a month remaining to national examination.

Majority of the candidates across the country were ranked below average in the tests that were done at the end of 2020 to serve as a precursor to what should be expected in the national exam.

According to KNEC, more than half of the 1.1 million candidates scored less than 50% in each subject.

The subjects that registered poor performance (less than 50%) include English Language, Kiswahili, Mathematics and Science.

In terms of counties, only 17 out of 47 managed an average of 50%.
