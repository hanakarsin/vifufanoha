Four family members who were murdered in cold blood in Kiambu county have been laid to rest in Karura, Kiambaa sub county.

Nicholas Njoroge, his wife Wanjiku Njoroge, their son and a nephew were found killed in their house on Tuesday, January 5, were sent off in an emotional ceremony on Saturday, January 16.

A funeral programme for their burial said the quadruple who were murdered alongside a farmworker were eliminated by unknown people who gained access to his home.

"On the night of Tuesday, January 5, unknown people gained access to the late Nicholas Njoroge’s family home in Ndundu, Karura Village and murdered all the family members who were present. "Nicholas Njoroge was killed together with his wife Ann Wanjiku Njoroge, last born son Christian Njenga and adopted nephew Maxwell Njenga. Earlier in the night, they had accessed the adjacent compound and killed the late James Kinyanjui, a mason, who was guarding a construction site," it added.

The couple sustained what looked like deep machete cuts with Njoroge's abdomen badly mutilated.

Main suspect arrested

Njoroge's eldest son Lawrence Waruinge, 22, has since confessed he killed his four family members and the worker.

Lawrence, who is the prime suspect in the brutal murder led detectives to the spot in Mai Mahiu where he disposed the murder weapons.

"This afternoon, the prime suspect in the murder, 22-year-old Lawrence Simon Warunge, has led detectives to the spot where he disposed the murder weapons and other key exhibits at a pit latrine in Mai Mahiu," a statement by the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) stated on Saturday, January 9.

The suspect was said to have had a strained relationship with his parents.
