President Samia Suluhu of Tanzania will address a joint sitting of parliament in Nairobi on Wednesday, May 5, during her first visit to Kenya as a head of state.

Suluhu is set to make her inaugural visit to Kenya as Tanzania's head of state on Tuesday, May 4, and will be received by President Uhuru Kenyatta at State House Nairobi.

The Speaker of the National Assembly released a statement on Monday, May 2, and informed lawmakers from both Houses Suluhu will make an address at the National Assembly chambers at 2.30pm on Wednesday.

"Notice is given to all Members of National Assembly, pursuant to Standing Order 25 (1) the Speaker of the National Assembly in consultation with the Speaker of the Senate has arranged for a joint sitting of Parliament to be held on Wednesday, May 5, 2021, at 2.30pm at the National Assembly chambers, parliament building for an address by Tanzania president Samia Suluhu,” read the notice.

As earlier reported, state house Spokesperson Kanze Dena said details about Suluhu's visit would be divulged in due time, but reports indicated the trip would have diplomatic significance.

The Tanzanian leader will visit the country nearly five years after her predecessor, the late John Pombe Magufuli, made a similar visit.

In her speech to the Tanzanian parliament last week, Suluhu reiterated her commitment to repairing the diplomatic ties that may have been severed in the past regimes.

On Sunday, April 11, the head of state made her maiden trip to Uganda, where President Yoweri Museveni received her.
