Twist braids

Currently, Senegalese twists are a popular protective hairstyle consisting of two-stranded twists with hair extensions used to add volume as well as length. The extensions can be done in two ways: using the twisting technique, they can be attached from the beginning or before the twists begin they can be added to the head with one-inch braids.

These twists take approximately six hours and can last for two to three months if well taken care of. In fact with the right supplies and practice, you can end up doing the Senegalese twists for yourself. The style of braiding is a beautiful technique that looks great in both natural and relaxed hair textures.

Below is a step by step straightforward tutorial on how to achieve this versatile braiding style:

Tools Required:

-Tail Comb

-Synthetic Hair Extension

-Two small hair clips

The Braiding process

1. Assuming your hair is washed and blow dried, do not use any oil on your hair before you start braiding. Comb a small section of your hair and use small clips to separate the remaining hair and keep it out of the way for braiding to achieve neat braids.

2. Begin twisting with a tail comb every section to the right until they all start to coil about an inch in length. In case you are adding the hair extensions, take a long piece that will be enough to twist till the ends of the hair to your desired length.

3. The next step is to make the two coiled sections of your hair and then take the extension hair in your right hand and pass it over the piece in your left hand once. Afterwards, coil the sections to the right again then take the piece over in your right hand and under the piece in your left hand.

4. Continue coiling and twisting the sections of hair until you get to the ends and your entire head is braided. Once done, dip the ends into a bowl of hot water which will help in sealing the ends and prevent them from untwisting.

Maintenance of Senegalese Twists

-Wash the braids at least once a week but only with diluted shampoo. Avoid using creamy conditioners on the braids because they may cause knotting and at the base of your braids.

-Use light oil or even liquid leave-in conditioner to ensure your scalp is healthy and moisturized at least twice a week.

Long box braids

Most people opt for box braids because they have a gorgeous hairstyle base and can be enhanced with simple styling solutions. Long box braids offer a wider range of hairstyles, and their main attraction is the length.

The easiest and most common style you can do with long box braids is a high ponytail wrapped a bun which works best if you have a formal event coming up such as a graduation or wedding. You can also be elegant with a double bun which is suitable for work and a night out as well.

Small box braids

Two hairstyles that never seem to go out of style or become outdated are the small box braids and bob haircuts. One of the advantages of small box braids is that they are light because of their short length.

Cornrow braid hairstyles

Cornrows are a cornerstone for children, women, and even men in the African American community. Cornrows are often in straight and simple lines as the name suggests, but they can also be done in curvilinear designs.
