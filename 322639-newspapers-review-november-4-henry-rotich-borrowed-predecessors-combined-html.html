Newspapers review for November 4: Henry Rotich borrowed more than all his predecessors combined

April 2024 Â· 3 minute read

The five national dailies on Monday, November 4, focused on the upcoming Kibra by-election slated for Thursday, November 7.

The kick-off of the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) examination today is also another story widely covered in the newspapers.

1. The Star

The daily reports that 50 KCPE invigilators were found to have infringed some exam regulations and the Teachers Service Commission (TSC) is set to take disciplinary action against them.

Some of the malpractices they were accused of committing include omission of labelling exam papers, late arrival at the exam centre, drunkness during the exam among other offences.

The teachers received the show cause letters and are expected to give satisfactory explanations within 14 days.

This comes as the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) examinations is set to kick off today.

Invigilators and all stakeholders have been asked to administer the exam with utmost fidelity.

2. Daily Nation

The daily reveals that President Uhuru Kenyatta and Orange Democratic Movement(ODM) party leader Raila Odinga have been reaching out to governors for their support in the Building Bridges Initiatives (BBI).

The duo hopes they can capitalise on the governors' influence in the grassroots to rally Kenyans behind the BBI.

This comes amid tensions rising on the contents of the initiative which reportedly supports the parliamentary system of voting.

Mt Kenya leaders have vowed to reject it if it threatens the one man one vote system.

3. Taifa Leo

The daily reported of the ostracisation Wiper party leader Kalonzo Musyoka has been facing from Ukambani governors Charity Ngilu and Alfred Mutua, who were recently spotted drumming up support for the ODM candidate, Bernard Okoth for the upcoming KIbra by-elections.

Kalonzo had earlier said his party is in support of the Ford Kenya candidate Khamisi Butichi but the leaders from his political stronghold did not follow suit.

Mutua's party, Maendeleo ChapChap recently won the Mutonguni ward by-election further proving the weakening influence of the Wiper party in the region.

Makueni Members of County Assembly have been condemning Kalonzo for supporting Governor Kivutha Kibwana's policies.

4. The Standard

The paper gives information on the ballooning debt-ceiling which could see the country crash according to the current Acting Treasury Cabinet Secretary, Ukur Yattani.

Former Treasury CS Henry Rotich reportedly borrowed more than all his 14 predecessors combined and left the country in a scathing debt crisis.

In five years, he borrowed KSh 3.1 trillion and signed off loans that could damage the country.

The Director of Public Prosecutions said he flouted procurement rules and abused the oath of office by defrauding the state.

5. People Daily

The daily reports that 15 new cases of cancer are reported every month in Mombasa county.

Mombasa County health chief officer Khadija Shikely said that despite the rise, Mombasa residents still fear to go for screening of the disease.

The health chief officer said breast cancer was more prevalent in women while men were suffering from prostate cancer accounting for 16.3 per cent prevalence rate.
