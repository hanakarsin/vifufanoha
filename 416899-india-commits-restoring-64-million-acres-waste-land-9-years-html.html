India Commits to Restoring 64 Million Acres of Wasteland within 9 Years

May 2024 · 3 minute read
  • India's prime minister, Narendra Modi, said the culture of land degradation had posed a great challenge not only to India but to rest of the world as well
  • The PM said he was keen on using international fora to spread the anti-land degradation agenda
  • As the Indian government embarked on the restoration of land, Modi said the move would also be friendly in theindigenous settings

India's prime minister, Narendra Modi, has reiterated that by 2030, his country would have reclaimed approximately 64 million acres of wasted land.

Speaking at the UN High-Level Dialogue on Desertification, Land Degradation and Drought, Modi said the restoration will help contribute to India’s commitment to absorb up to three billion tons of carbon from the atmosphere, hence contributing to lowering carbon emissions.

“We are on track to achieve our national commitment of land degradation neutrality. We are working towards restoring 64 million acres of wasteland by 2013,” said the PM.

According to Modi, land is the fundamental building block for supporting all lives and livelihoods. Unfortunately, land degradation, he said, affects over two-thirds of the world today.

He held that unless something is done, land degradation will erode the very foundations of societies, economies, food security, health, safety and quality of life.

The PM emphasized that land degradation had over the years become a special challenge in Third World economies.

He recalled the 2019 Delhi declaration two years ago, which pushed for better access to land.

What is land degradation?

According to the United Nation’s Food and Agriculture Organisation (FAO), land degradation is the temporary or permanent lowering of the land's productive capacity.

The UN estimates about 3.2 billion people around the world face the risk of land degradation effects.

A previous study by the Indian government found that land degradation led to roughly 2.5 per cent loss of the country’s economic output between 2014 and 2015.

As a result, Modi said he would take the lead role in highlighting land degradation issues at the international fora.

He opined its the world's sacred duty to leave behind a healthy planet for future generations.

The Indian government figures showed nearly 97 hectares of land in the country, close to the size of Northern Europe, had been degraded.

Human causes, agriculture, and urbanisation were listed as the major causes of land degradation in India and the rest of the world.
