Nakuru woman who stripped twice in court wins land case after 10 years legal battle

June 2024 · 2 minute read

- Jepkosgei had been expecting a court verdict over the 10-year-old case

- The judge deferred the ruling saying he needed more time to check on facts of the case

- She became angry and started shouting in the courtroom while stripping

Angelina Jepkosgei was all smiles after a court in Nakuru ruled she was the rightful owner of a controversial land.

On several occasions, Jepkemoi got agitated and stripped naked when Justice Anthony Ndung’u postponed sessions as he needed more time to look into the facts of the case that has dragged since 2009.

This was after the judge said he would give the ruling on Thursday, January 17, but said he could do it on Wednesday, January 30 prompting Jepkosgei to undress.

On Thursday, January 31, members of the public, most of whom were family members thronged the courtroom awaiting the elusive justice to take its course.

And soon it was time for Justice Ndung'u to give the verdict in the silent court, Jepkosgei pensively followed minute by minute development.

“I accordingly vacate the orders restraining Jepkemoi, the administrator of the estate of Chemerus from distributing parcel number Nakuru/Municipality Block 13/312.The administrator is at liberty to apply in respect of furtherance of the administration of the estate herein," ruled Ndung'u.

The woman has been involved in the case since 2009 seeking to administer a piece of land her mother jointly owned with Robert Tanui.
