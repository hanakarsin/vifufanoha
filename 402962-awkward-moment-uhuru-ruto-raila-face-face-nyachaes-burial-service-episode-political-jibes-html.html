Awkward moment Uhuru, Ruto, Raila come face-to-face at Nyachae's burial service

- Uhuru, for the first time, last week, asked Ruto to resigned for complaining about the shortfalls of the government he was part of

President Uhuru Kenyatta, his deputy William Ruto and ODM leader Raila Odinga have for the first time in weeks shared a podium.

The two leaders, who their relationship has been deteriorating daily, on Monday, February 15, sat next to each other at the VIP dais at Gusii Stadium during the funeral service of former cabinet minister Simeon Nyachae.

Uhuru arrived at the venue at around 10.30am aboard his official Kenya Air Force chopper where he joined Ruto and ODM leader Raila Odinga who had come earlier.

A close look at Uhuru and Ruto showed an atmosphere of awkwardness and tension. The duo hardly looked at each other as it used to happen during their first term in office when they could whisper into each other's ear and perhaps crack jokes whenever they shared a podium.

Close to Uhuru and Ruto, sat Raila, who does not see eye to eye with the second-in-command.

Uhuru and Ruto bromance

The bromance that punctuated Uhuru and Ruto's relationship started fading away in the early days of their second term in office.

The two fell out after the March 2018 handshake between Uhuru and Raila. The truce sparked feelings of betrayal from the DP who accused Raila of entering the government through the back door and causing turmoil.

Allies of the DP have also come forth and accused the president of backtracking on his promise to support Ruto to ascend to state house after his tenure expires in 2022 in favour of Raila.

Resign

This has caused the DP to engage the campaign gear to shore up his chances of succeeding Uhuru. His actions have, however, irked the president who recently asked him (Ruto) to resign.

Uhuru lashed out at Ruto for criticising the government and at the same time claiming credit for the good things the government has done.

The president also left tongues wagging after saying he would not "leave the country's leadership in the hands of a thief and an oppressor".

Ruto's response

Ruto fired back at the president, stating he would not resign but will continue serving as the deputy president.

"I will continue to work as the deputy president of Kenya because that is my place in the constitution. He is the one who calls the shot in the government, and I have never done contrary to what he commands because I respect him. I have not complained even after some of my duties were reassigned to other people," said Ruto.

During the Monday funeral service, Kisii deputy governor Joash Maangi and South Mugirango MP Sylvanus Osoro, allies of Ruto, were arrested as they were waiting to welcome the DP.
