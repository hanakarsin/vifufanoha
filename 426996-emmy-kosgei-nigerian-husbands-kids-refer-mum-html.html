Award-winning Kenyan gospel singer Emmy Kosgei is happily married to a Nigerian pastor Anselm Madubuko.

The 41-year-old singer married the Nigerian preacher in August 2013 in a colourful wedding at Windsor in Nairobi, Kenya. In an interview with The Standard, Emmy revealed that she did not date Madubuko before they decided to walk down the aisle. She did not know him much.

Emmy and Madubuko don't have any children, but he has three from his first marriage. The general overseer of Revival Ministries has three children with his late wife Connie. He has two grandchildren.

Emmy talks about meeting hubby's children

The Taunet Nelel hitmaker said she relates well with her stepchildren.

"I had not encountered his children personally, so it was by faith, but I was not scared. I had peace in my heart. It ended up working out beautifully," she told The Standard.

It was a few months after their mother had passed away.

"Those kids had never lived in Nigeria, so we came at the same time. That exposure was so powerful. When you go into a marriage that has children it is usually not easy to strike a balance but quietly we've been able to balance that, the ministry, and my own ministry," she said.

Emmy also disclosed that Madubuko's children respect her.

"They call me mum. They give me my place. The first daughter has kids so we have grandchildren. They were born when I was there and they call me grandma. I'm in their lives," she said.

Emmy and Madubuko celebrate eight years in marriage

On August 31, 2021, the singer and her famous hubby celebrated their eighth wedding anniversary with glowing messages.

"On a day like this, I said yes! Happy Anniversary to us! Eight years of bliss, of wifing this great general, who has endlessly thieved (stolen) this #nubianqueens' heart. Thank you Jesus! This ninth year we step into oil and butter...in Jesus mighty name," Emmy wrote.
