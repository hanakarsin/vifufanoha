Baringo county's rejection of BBI big blow to Gideon, win for Ruto

Baringo Senator Gideon Moi is one of the chief proponents of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) amendment bill 2020 in the country.

The KANU party leader is also among the top political leaders featuring on the list of probable and potential 2022 presidential candidates. In fact, he has declared he will be contesting for the top seat.

His immediate opponent in the State House race is Deputy President William Ruto, a political rivalry from the expansion and vote-rich Rift Valley region.

With Kenyan politics being 'naturally' tribal and regional-based, the young Moi has no option but to take on Ruto and fend off his political dominance in Rift Valley.

However, on a few occasions and moments that have presented themselves for the Baringo senator to show his muscles and supremacy, the son of Kenya's second president has fallen short of the strength and surrendered the fight to the DP.

The recent move by Baringo county assembly to overwhelmingly reject the BBI bill which he has been marketing across the county is one of those lowest moments the soft-spoken KANU leader has suffered.

Early signs

Although the bill was shot down in a chaotic session that made it difficult for one to tell whether or not due process was followed, the writing was already on the wall that BIBI would face a serious hurdle in Baringo.

It was on this basis and growing concerns that secretary-general Raphael Tuju called all Jubilee MCAs from the county to Nairobi for a special meeting held at the party's Pangani head office.

The SG spent hours talking to the ward reps and urging them to join hands with their KANU counterparts to pass the BBI bill.

Tuju even gave them the freedom to ask or tell him anything they felt pressing.

The question that many people asked themselves was, why did the SG choose to address Baringo county MCAs alone when several other counties under the fold of Jubilee Party had tabled the bill as well?

The possible logical conclusion could be the party was worried things might go south because of the existing sibling rivalry between Ruto and Gideon.

This was evident when a section of MCAs seized the rare opportunity extended to them by Tuju to raise any pressing issues with him.

In an amateur video that surfaced online moments after the meeting, the SG was put to task to explain why the party was targetting some members, particularly those allied to Ruto.

They also wanted to know the root cause of the existing bad blood between President Uhuru Kenyatta and the DP.

Although the Jubilee spokesman tried to downplay the whole thing by assuring them all was well and that Uhuru and Ruto would make peace just like the former did with Opposition chief Raila Odinga, the ward reps did not appear convinced.

Game of numbers

Baringo county assembly has a total of 45 MCAs. Out of this, Jubilee has 21 elected ward reps and 10 nominated, adding up to 31. KANU has seven elected MCAs and four nominated, bringing the total number to 11.

The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) has one while Independent candidates are two.

The majority of the Jubilee MCAs in the county are allied to Ruto who is opposed to the BBI bill.

Here is where Gideon started losing the fight. As a party leader and a potential presidential candidate with massive financial muscles and countrywide network, the son of Moi would have secured at least double the number of MCAs his party got in 2017.

It speaks volumes when the party you lead cannot get significant support and representation in your own backyard.

It is worrying when your arch-rival beats you in your own home turf.

Although it is not clear home many Jubilee MCAs are allied to Ruto in Baringo, the chaotic pronouncement by the Speaker that 31 ward reps had voted to reject the bill against 11 who endorsed it conveys a strong message about Ruto's deeply rooted political tentacles in the county.

Being his home county, Gideon must pull up his socks if indeed he intends to run against the DP in 2022.

Lost opportunity

Be it as it may, the Baringo senator, by all standards, failed to seize a golden opportunity to outshine the DP.

With 45 MCAs, whom he interacts with quite often by the virtue of being the county senator, Gideon had a good chance to showcase his lobbying skills and woo the assembly to pass the handshake document.

It is worth noting that KANU signed a post-election coalition agreement with Jubilee, making it even easier for him to engage MCAs elected on the ruling party ticket and whip them to support BBI.

The fist-fights and running battles that were witnessed in the assembly would not have been seen had he demonstrated good leadership and lobbying skills.

It should concern Gideon that the MCAs shot down the bill despite the recent move by President Uhuru to dangle the carrot in form of a car grant, a demand they have been crying for since 2013.

For a presidential candidate and a man sitting at the country's high table of political kinship, the passage of such a bill, under the prevailing favourable political circumstances brought about by the handshake, should not have been a struggle, leave alone a loss.
