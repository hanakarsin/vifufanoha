CS Amina Mohamed finally admitted to the bar 7 years after gazettement

Chief Justice and the president of the Supreme Court David Maraga has presided over the admission of 198 new advocates to the bar.

Among the new advocates was the Sports Cabinet Secretary Amina Mohamed who revealed her admission was gazetted in 2013 but opted to delay for personal reasons.

"On the second day of admissions today, I presided over the admission of another 96 advocates, among them CS Amina Mohammed, bringing the total to 198 new members who have joined the Bar. My hearty congratulations," Maraga stated.

The CS earned a Master of Laws (LLM) in International Law from Kiev University and postgraduate diploma in International Relations from the University of Oxford.

Through a Fellowship at the United Nations Institute for Training and Research (UNITAR), she also followed several training courses on international law.

Amina had not been able to practise law in Kenya even though she had obtained diploma in law from Kenya School of Law which grants one the permission to become the high court advocate.

"Thank you very much. I am most grateful to God for this opportunity even though it comes seven years after gazettement in 2013. Thank you to the team for freeing up time for me to finally sign the roll," said an excited CS.
